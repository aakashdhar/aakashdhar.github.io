Introducing Mistral Small 4
Mistral AI
just now
Mistral Small 4 collapses three previously separate models — Magistral (reasoning), Devstral (coding), and Pixtral (vision) — into a single 119B MoE model with only 6B active parameters per token. The standout engineering choice is a configurable `reasoning_effort` parameter that lets you dial between low-latency chat and deep chain-of-thought without switching models. Benchmarks show it matches or beats GPT-OSS 120B on reasoning tasks while producing 20–40% shorter outputs, which matters directly for inference cost at scale.

EMO: Pretraining mixture of experts for emergent modularity
Hugging Face
just now
AllenAI's EMO is a 1B-active/14B-total MoE that learns domain-coherent expert groups end-to-end — no predefined domain labels required. By constraining all tokens in a document to share an expert pool during training, EMO achieves near full-model performance using just 12.5% of experts on a given task, while standard MoEs collapse to near-random under the same conditions. We get semantic expert clusters (Health, US Politics, Film) instead of the syntactic noise (prepositions, definite articles) that standard MoE routing produces.
